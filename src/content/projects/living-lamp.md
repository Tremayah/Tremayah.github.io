An ambitious first-year finale: autonomous movement, hand gesture recognition, and a design philosophy borrowed from bioluminescent organisms and Bungie's Marathon.

## Research

Library books — _Bio-Design_ by William Myers and _Art + Science Now_ by Stephen Wilson — opened up bioluminescent bacteria and living leather as reference points. Marathon shaped the aesthetic: raw functionality, artificial life, factory-fresh surfaces. The goal was something with undeniable originality rather than generic design.

## Electronics

No Arduino experience. Minimal circuit knowledge. Almost no metalworking background. Starting with an Arduino MKR1000, the first LED flashed. Then an external LED wired through a pin. Then a servo motor — initially mistaken for a stepper, quickly corrected. Each failure was a lesson learned in a week.

![Hardware prototyping](https://raphaelmurraybrowne.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/img_1537.jpeg)

## Metalwork

Without lathe induction, early work focused on cutting aluminium bars and steel pipe. Welding failed — different melting temperatures. Solution: threading the pipes to screw into aluminium. A tap snapped mid-thread; the design pivoted to aluminium pipe axle with pre-manufactured holes. Loose connections were resolved with metal epoxy overnight.

![Workshop construction](https://raphaelmurraybrowne.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/img_1679.jpeg)

![Steel and aluminium assembly](https://raphaelmurraybrowne.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/img_1694.jpeg)

## Design

Five base designs — flat, wall clamp, wearable, rail, and clamp. Clamp won for weight and portability. A dual-cylinder rotating axle handles the hinges; a locking bolt pushing rubber onto the axle provides adjustable friction. Five sketch pages, two 3D models, and extensive consultation before the hinge geometry resolved.

![Form research sketches](https://raphaelmurraybrowne.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/form-research-2.png)

## Outcome

Fabric cover prevents sheet metal rust. Battery-powered torch behind translucent film for the presentation. Vice marks left visible on the steel pipes — aligned with the raw functionality philosophy. The interactive and AI-responsive components remain incomplete. This project is far from over.
