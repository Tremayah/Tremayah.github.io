A personal furniture study. The tabletop is built up from stacked layers of wood, so the grain and the steps between layers read like the contour lines of a topographic map. A clear top then seals the landscape flat, turning a relief you'd normally only look at into a surface you can actually use.

I modelled the table in Rhino and rendered it in Blender, dropping it into a living-room scene to test how the piece sits in a real space — the layered top against a patterned rug, with soft daylight raking across the steps to catch the level changes.
